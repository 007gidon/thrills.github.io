Rosemary's Baby

"Rosemary's Baby" (1968), masterfully directed by Roman Polanski and based on Ira Levin's novel, intricately weaves the tale of Mia Farrow's character—a pregnant young wife in Manhattan unraveling a sinister Satanic cult plot involving her elderly neighbors. As the narrative probes into themes of paranoia, women's liberation, Catholicism, and the occult, the film navigates the iconic streets of New York City, although predominantly filmed in Los Angeles. Its critical acclaim and substantial box office success, surpassing $30 million, catapulted Ruth Gordon to a Best Supporting Actress triumph at both the Academy Awards and Golden Globes. Revered as a pinnacle in horror cinema, its induction into the National Film Registry in 2014 cements its lasting influence and cultural resonance.
