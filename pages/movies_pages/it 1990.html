IT 1990

"It" is a 1990 two-part miniseries based on Stephen King's novel, directed by Tommy Lee Wallace and adapted by Lawrence D. Cohen. The story revolves around The Lucky Seven, a group of outcast kids who confront a predatory monster capable of transforming into their worst fears, often taking the form of a demonic clown named Pennywise. Filmed over three months in New Westminster, British Columbia, with a budget of $12 million, the miniseries was a major success for ABC despite mixed critical reviews. It garnered 30 million viewers for its two parts and received nominations for several awards, winning an Emmy for its music composition and an Eddie Award for editing. Tim Curry's portrayal of Pennywise has become iconic, earning praise for its scare factor. The miniseries has continued to captivate audiences and has even inspired documentary films exploring its production and legacy.

  • Supernatural Horror:

    In "It," the supernatural subgenre revolves around Pennywise, a predatory monster capable of transforming into its victims' worst fears.
