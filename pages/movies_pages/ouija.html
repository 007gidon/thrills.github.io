Ouija: Origin of Evil

"Ouija: Origin of Evil" is a gripping 2016 American supernatural horror film, skillfully helmed by director and editor Mike Flanagan, who also co-wrote the screenplay alongside Jeff Howard. Serving as a prequel to the 2014 film "Ouija," the movie boasts a talented ensemble cast including Elizabeth Reaser, Lulu Wilson, Annalise Basso, and Henry Thomas. Set in 1967, the story revolves around a widow and her family who incorporate a Ouija board into their fake seance business, unwittingly inviting a malevolent spirit to possess their youngest daughter. Released by Universal Pictures in the United States on October 21, 2016, "Ouija: Origin of Evil" garnered widespread acclaim from both audiences and critics alike. With its chilling atmosphere, compelling performances, and skillful direction, the film captivated viewers and served as a significant improvement over its predecessor. Grossing over $81 million worldwide, it solidified its place as a standout entry in the supernatural horror genre, leaving a lasting impression on audiences with its haunting tale of terror and possession.
