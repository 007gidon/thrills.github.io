Barbarian

Barbarian is a 2022 American horror thriller film written and directed by Zach Cregger in his solo screen writing and directorial debut. It is produced by Arnon Milchan, Roy Lee, Raphael Margules, and J. D. Lifshitz. The film stars Georgina Campbell, Bill Skarsgård, and Justin Long. The plot sees a woman find out that the rental home she reserved has been accidentally double-booked by a man, not knowing of a dark secret within the dwelling. Barbarian premiered at San Diego Comic-Con on July 22, 2022, and was theatrically released in the United States on September 9, 2022, by 20th Century Studios. The film received largely positive reviews, with praise for Cregger's screenplay and direction as well as the cast performances. The film grossed over $45 million worldwide on a production budget of $4–4.5 million.

  • Psychological Horror:

    "Barbarian" (2022) immerses viewers in a profound exploration of psychological trauma experienced by its protagonists. The film weaves a gripping narrative, gradually unveiling the chilling consequences of extreme torment. Director Zach Cregger's masterful direction skillfully manipulates tension and fear, creating a suspenseful atmosphere that resonates deeply with the audience, leaving a lasting impact on their emotions and fears. The inclusion of talented actors, including Bill Skarsgård and Justin Long, adds to the film's unsettling nature, enhancing the overall experience. Despite its familiar horror elements, "Barbarian" stands out as a bold curiosity, with Cregger's artistic impulses and unique storytelling choices creating a metaphorical house of mirrors that is both disturbing and compelling.
