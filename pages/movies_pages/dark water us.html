Dark Water

"Dark Water" is a chilling 2005 American supernatural horror film directed by Walter Salles and written by Rafael Yglesias. A remake of the acclaimed 2002 Japanese film of the same name, it draws inspiration from Koji Suzuki's short story "Floating Water," the same mind behind the Ring trilogy. The star-studded cast includes Jennifer Connelly, Tim Roth, John C. Reilly, Pete Postlethwaite, Perla Haney-Jardine, Dougray Scott, and Ariel Gade. Released on July 8, 2005, "Dark Water" garnered $44.4–49.5 million worldwide. It stands as a gripping co-production between the United States and Japan, captivating audiences with its eerie tale of supernatural terror and psychological suspense.
