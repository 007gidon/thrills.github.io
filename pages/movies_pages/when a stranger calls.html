When A Stranger Calls

"When a Stranger Calls" (2006) is a captivating American psychological horror film that skillfully extends the tension of its predecessor, Fred Walton's 1979 horror classic. Directed by Simon West and featuring a talented ensemble cast including Camilla Belle, Brian Geraghty, Katie Cassidy, Tessa Thompson, and Clark Gregg, the film immerses viewers in a prolonged battle of wits between the babysitter and her mysterious tormentor. Building upon the cult status of the original film, this remake amplifies the atmosphere of fear and paranoia, drawing audiences into a gripping narrative that unfolds with chilling suspense. Despite receiving mixed reviews from critics, the film found success at the box office, grossing $67.1 million worldwide against a $15 million budget. Its ability to maintain tension and deliver a compelling exploration of psychological terror solidifies its place as a noteworthy entry in the genre.
